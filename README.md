# 7-1_FinalProjectMilestones

How do I approach designing software?
-What new design skills has your work on the project helped you to craft?
  I think most of all this project helped me really understand 3D visualization. I feel like I have become pretty adept at understanding abstract coordinates and relating that to generated objects in a 3D environment. This skill allowed me to recreate the scene and object at a time.
-What design process did you follow for your project work?
  At first I just built objects relative to others in the scene by sight, but as I continued I realized that more planning beforehand would have made the project easier. I had to adjust my design process as I went to compensate.
-How could tactics from your design approach be applied in future work?
  No matter what project is being developed, I believe having a strong plan is a key tactic to designing a strong project. Good planning makes the project come down to execution, rather than time or talent.

How do I approach developing programs?
-What new development strategies did you use while working on your 3D scene?
  I relied a lot on incremental building and rendering. I worked one mesh at a time confirming correct vertices and transformations, before applying texture. Once everything was correct I moved onto the next object.
-How did iteration factor into your development?
  Iteration was really import for designing complex objects. Constant micro adjustments to scale and coordinates were necessary to create each object correctly. This also helped me with the lighting as it took me a while to recreate the blue glow from the reference photo.
-How has your approach to developing code evolved throughout the milestones, which led you to the project’s completion?
  At first I wrote code line by line for every object, but eventually I realized that wasn't sustainable for the project. I created myself some templates which I could use to recreate objects quicker by filling in specific information. And once building a completed object it became easier to save the object entirely to reuse, rather than create it piece by piece again. 

How can computer science help me in reaching my goals?
-How do computational graphics and visualizations give you new knowledge and skills that can be applied in your future educational pathway?
  I am really interested in data visualization and this feels like a helpful stepping stone in that direction. While I don't think I will be rendering scenes like this, I think having the understanding of 3D spacial environments is really valuable. 
-How do computational graphics and visualizations give you new knowledge and skills that can be applied in your future professional pathway?
  At some point I would love to dabble in the game industry and graphics and visualizations are a really important part of games. I also think that more realistic job prospects for me exist in data analysis and visualization is a huge part of that. Many people have a hard time visualizing large amounts of raw data so presenting it as a visual representation is a strong asset in a business environment.
